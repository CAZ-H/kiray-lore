# The Fall of Civilization — A Brief History

When the Zorn arrived, they came with an ultimatum. Impressed by the vast industrial and technological success of Skiedonic civilization, and seeing an opportunity to further their own people, they demanded that Kiray surrender a portion of its population to be "put to work." In reality, this meant 80% of the global population. Kiray was given half a rotation about the sun to decide who would be sacrificed. The Zorn delivered their message with overwhelming displays of power, broadcasting their demands across the planet before departing for a neighboring star system.

A century prior, the Zorn had been rescued from their dying star by a technologically superior race, the Tox. Whether out of apathy or blind generosity, the Tox failed to consider the consequences of unleashing them upon the universe. They provided the Zorn with ships capable of subspace travel, the knowledge required to operate them, and the machines and resources necessary for their upkeep. They remained only long enough to convince the Zorn of their impending doom, instruct them on the basics of their newfound technology, and then bid them good luck.

To the Zorn, these so-called gifts were an insult. Their world was doomed, their survival now dependent on foreign machines they could neither understand nor build more of. In their eyes, the Tox had condemned them to extinction and then abandoned them to it. Enraged but unwilling to die, they adapted, using whatever means were necessary to seize control of their fate.

The Zorn were an intimidating species due to being physically immense, aggressive, and exceptionally long-lived, yet for all of their strength, they were also stunningly uncreative, lacking ingenuity and the ability to reason abstractly. They could not understand how this technology worked, but they could understand how it could be used. Their intelligence shone through mostly in the form of strategy for dominance, social manipulation, and brute force problem solving. While they were not capable of creating, they were very good at stealing, imitating, and modifying, and had the lifespan to store decades of practical wisdom. Fueled by the drive to survive and their indignant rage at the Tox, they spent their remaining decades on trial and error. Through sheer persistence, they succeeded in mastering the foreign ships, evacuating a portion of their population from their fate. With access to subspace travel, anything in the universe was theirs to take, their only goal now being to survive.

However, their vessels were not large enough, the Zorn required vast amounts of space to accommodate their massive forms. As their population grew, so did their need for both intellectual and physical labor to expand their fleet. As they traveled the stars in conquest, they collected technology and ships from other races and enslaved those races to maintain them, their fleet growing to be a terrifying mishmash of retrofitted spacecraft with stolen weapons technology crudely bolted on. Yet, despite their fervent warpath, they could not find a planet suitable for their biological needs. The artificial environments aboard their repurposed ships sustained them on their conquest.

The Greater Skiedonic Nation, space-faring within their local system and only just a century into the development of their World Ship program, had no means to oppose the Zorn's might. Any resistance would have simply ended in annihilation. Fearing the annihilation of their people and the promise of enslavement, in the knowledge that the Zorn could easily return time and time again to harvest their people for eternity, the leaders of the Nation made the decision to evacuate the most capable members of their race off-world in the six World Ships at their disposal, abandoning their homeworld in search of a distant habitable planet, and a hopeful fresh start.

Unlike the Zorn, the Skiedonic people had never been a violent species. Their civilization was built upon curiosity, cooperation, and a deep reverence for life and the stars. Their research focused on biology, life support, and space exploration, with no emphasis on warfare. Each World Ship was designed to sustain a population of several thousand indefinitely, but none were equipped with weaponry. Furthermore, their subspace travel technology was under-developed. From their own testing, they knew they could make one semi-stable jump out of the system, but they had not yet perfected the technology; the subspace drives built into each of the world ships were all different experimental models, transplanted from other test vessels, and may not necessarily send each world ship the same distance, or work at all. This meant that the evacuation of Kiray would shatter the Skiedonic race through space, and the rest of the journey of each individual world ship could span thousands of generations.

When the Nation fled, over 60% of the global Skiedonic population was left behind. Those living in the core of the Nation; the scientists, engineers, and intellectual elite, capable of assuring their survival for centuries, were prioritized for evacuation, while the vast majority of the population, particularly those in outlying regions, had no means of escape. There simply wasn't enough room for more.

When the Zorn returned on schedule, they were enraged to find only a fraction of their intended harvest remaining. In retaliation, they abducted and enslaved the entire remaining population, sweeping across the planet with stolen advanced technology, leaving few behind. The Zorn spent some time on Kiray afterwards, methodically peeling apart each developed industrial city to salvage the Skiedonic machines and technology left behind, even having the enslaved Skiedons do a large portion of the work. When everything of use was stripped, Kiray was razed as punishment, its ransacked cities and settlements reduced to blackened rubble until no trace of developed Skiedonic civilization remained. Still unsatisfied, the Zorn unleashed a bioweapon stolen from another conquered race upon the ruins of the Skiedonic capital before departing.

Though the flora and fauna of Kiray recovered quickly, Skiedonic civilization did not. Those who survived; scattered individuals who had hidden in caves or underground, eventually banded together, becoming the Remnants. The obliteration was so complete that little remained of the Greater Skiedonic Nation's achievements, culture, or technology. The Remnants now are left only with a language that distorts with each generation it's passed on to, and only the most important cornerstones of their former culture. A few relics of their past lay buried in deep research facilities, but most of their technological heritage was lost.

The bioweapon the Zorn unleashed, though originally designed for an entirely different world, was prolific and deadly. It was incapable of surviving in the direct light of Kiray's sun, but thrived in dark recesses. After the Zorn delivered it via an explosive capsule filled with spores, it spread aggressively through the corpse of the city center, rooting into porous surfaces, the dirt, and plant or animal life, proliferating in building interiors and exploding unchecked through the various tunnels of the shipping network, rapidly spreading across the formerly civilized side of the planet. Anyone who had remained hidden within the husk of the city was infected and gradually turned thrall, bodies warped, skin blackened, and minds taken by a madness that drove them after any sign of life. Beneath the surface it flourished. A creeping, virulent fungus spreading through the underground with its ghostly white mycelium veins weaving through the soil where no sunlight reached. Infected caverns were coated in a blanket of white mycelium and dripped with a black, tar-like substance. The stale air clouded with infectious spores, waiting for an unlucky creature to wander inside. Over time, this virulent plague became a global scourge that drove the remaining Skiedonic people into the trees, sowing superstition wherever it was found.

Thousands of years have passed since then, and neither the Greater Skiedonic Nation nor the Zorn have returned. In the absence of their once-great civilization, Skiedonic society has reverted to tribal settlements. With so much destruction and without widespread communication or trade, knowledge of the old world has faded with each generation. Only the most practical skills and the most terrifying legends have survived the test of time. The culture of the past is gone, replaced by a new way of life shaped by struggle and isolation. The strongest enduring remnant of the old world is the Skiedonic language, a fractured, tattered version of what it once was, still recognizable but changed.

Given enough time, it's possible this new world may rise to greatness once more.
