title: Tenit
---

Tenits are members of the [[Tinit#Taxonomy|true Tinit]] family and are the largest terrestrial members of that family. They are differentiated by their size, burrowing lifestyle, predatory diet, and appearance.

Where [[Tinit|Tinits]] and [[Tonit|Tonits]] are small and highly social, Tenits are medium-sized, solitary, and keep strictly to their own kind, to the point of hunting and eating their smaller relatives.

## Morphology

### General Anatomy

A Tenit is a horizontally oriented bipedal creature with a short tail and a forwards-pointed head, exhibiting a [[Kirian_Physiology#Pure Biped|pure biped body plan]] with a [[Kirian_Physiology#Continuous Structure|continuous body structure]].

Tenits share the general anatomy of [[Tinit|Tinits]], but are several times larger, on average around 45 centimeters long and 20 centimeters wide and tall, making Tinits and Tonits almost half as large by comparison.

### Limbs and Movement

**Legs:** Like other true Tinits, Tenits are [[Kirian_Physiology#Horizontal Hexapod|Kirian ungulates]], but their three toes are slightly more splayed, spreading their weight and improving stability in loose soil, as well as their ability to burst into a chasing sprint.

### Head and Sensory Systems

**Beak:** Tenit beaks bear more pronounced serrations than those of Tinits, along with pronounced ridges down their length, suiting them to catching and holding prey and shredding meat as readily as scavenging.

**Whiskers:** Their [[Kirian_Physiology#Facial Sensory Structures|whiskers]] are far more pronounced than those of Tinits, tuned to sense vibration and movement in the ground beneath them as they navigate underground and the surface.

**Chin Ridges:** Tenits bear pronounced ridges along the chin, a facial feature absent in Tinits and Tonits.

### Skin and Coloration

In place of the [[Tinit#Skin and Coloration|blue skin and red carapace]] of Tinits, Tenits have orange skin marked with dark blue, and a dark blue carapace. As in [[Tonit|Tonits]], the carapace's color is deepened by the elevated copper of their heavily carnivorous diet.

Their carapace is aggressively ridged, more pronounced than the bumps and ridges characteristic of Tinit carapaces.

## Behavior

Unlike the colonial Tinits and Tonits, Tenits are solitary or live in small packs of two to three, and keep strictly to their own kind. They do not associate with Tinit colonies; where their ranges overlap, they hunt and eat both Tinits and Tonits.

In general, Tenits are far more aggressive and predatory than either Tinits or Tonits.

Otherwise, Tenits behave similarly to [[Tinit#Behavior|Tinits]].

### Foraging and Feeding Habits

Tenits are medium-sized scavengers with a strong predatory streak. Alongside scavenging carrion and detritus, they actively hunt live prey, most notably their smaller relatives, [[Tinit|Tinits]] and [[Tonit|Tonits]].

Otherwise, Tenits forage and feed similarly to [[Tinit#Foraging and Feeding Habits|Tinits]].

## Lifespan

The average lifespan for a Tenit is around 8–11 Kirian years in the wild, longer than that of its smaller relatives, and it can live as long as 14 Kirian years in the absence of predation. Because Tenits are larger, live underground, and are themselves predators, they face far less predation than the small colonial scavengers, and so live out much more of their natural lifespan, with the wild average sitting closer to that ceiling. As in Tonits, however, their heavily carnivorous diet imposes higher oxidative stress, which keeps the ceiling from rising as far as their size alone might otherwise allow.

## Distribution and Habitat

Tenits are found in the [[Tinit#Distribution and Habitat|same broad environments as Tinits]], but spend most of their lives underground, emerging to hunt and scavenge. They favor ground soft enough to burrow through, and concentrate where Tinits, Tonits, and other small prey are plentiful.
