Tonits are members of the [[Tinit#Taxonomy|true Tinit]] family, differentiated by certain diet adaptations, behavioral tendencies, and their appearance. 

It is thought that they arose from a common mutation which found its own niche.

## Morphology

### General Anatomy

A Tonit is a horizontally oriented bipedal creature with a short tail and a forwards-pointed head, exhibiting a [[Kirian_Physiology#Pure Biped|pure biped body plan]] with a [[Kirian_Physiology#Continuous Structure|continuous body structure]]. Tonits are slightly smaller than Tinits despite their more aggressive nature, giving them a slight advantage for making quick escapes. They are around 16 centimeters long and 8 centimeters wide and tall, on average.

Their anatomy is almost identical to [[Tinit|Tinits]] in every way.

### Skin and Coloration

Tonits differ from tinits primarily in coloration and the shape of the carapace on their backs.

Instead of the [[Tinit#Skin and Coloration|distinctive blue skin and red carapace]] that Tinits bear, Tonits exhibit red skin and black or dark-red carapaces and tail markings. The red skin comes from a mutation that Tinits are also capable of expresing, which solidified into a common trait in this species. The darker carapace is a byproduct of the elevated dietary copper found in their altered, more carnivorous diet.

Tonit carapaces are also more likely to bear spikes and spiney protrusions, rather than the bumps and ridges characteristic of Tinit carapaces. This is thought to be a defensive adaptation. 

## Behavior

Tonits will often join rich Tinit colonies in regions where Tonit population is low, but they prefer colonies of their own species as their diets only partially overlap, and crossbreeding is not often successful.

In general, Tonits are more aggressive than Tinits.

Otherwise, Tonits behave similarly to [[Tinit#Behavior|Tinits.]]

### Foraging and Feeding Habits

Tonits differ from Tinits in their diets primarily, biasing strongly towards sources of protein through a higher propensity to carnivory and insectivory. They have been known to disproportionately prey upon the eggs and young of certain ground-nesting species in the regions where they are most common.

Otherwise, Tonits forage and feed similarly to [[Tinit#Foraging and Feeding Habits|Tinits.]]

## Lifespan

The average lifespan for a Tonit is 4-6 Kirian years in the wild, but they can live as long as 9 Kirian years in the absence of predation. This is shorter than Tinits and is thought to be due to higher oxidative stress brought on by their more carnivorous diet.

## Distribution and Habitat

Tonits are generally found in the [[Tinit#Distribution and Habitat|same environments as Tinits]], but concentrate more heavily in regions where small ground-nesting species live.
